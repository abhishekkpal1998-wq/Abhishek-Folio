Case study 01 · Aerohyre

Building India’s aviation hiring growth engine from zero.

Scaled a first-mover aviation job platform from 0 to 165K+ MAUs by building the acquisition, analytics, SEO, and CRO engine from scratch.

Challenge No category awareness, no historical CAC, marketplace cold start.
Strategy Acquire job seekers first, build trust, then scale acquisition.
Outcome 165K+ MAUs, 8K+ sign-ups, improved ROAS and CAC.

Business problem

Aerohyre was not just launching a product — it was creating a category. Aviation hiring was fragmented across generic job boards, referrals, and informal networks.

My thinking

The key decision was sequencing. I focused first on candidate liquidity because job-seeker traction would become the proof needed to later acquire employers.

Execution

Google Search, Meta, YouTube, retargeting, SEO landing pages, role-specific messaging, GA4/GTM tracking, UTM discipline, and weekly funnel reporting.

Experiments

CTA copy, search match types, form simplification, role-specific landing pages, and retargeting sequences.

What failed

Broad-match keywords wasted spend early. Generic job-portal messaging felt undifferentiated. Cold campaigns without retargeting underperformed.

Case study 02 · Google QLED TV

Launching Google QLED TV with creators + performance marketing.

Built a launch motion using creator-led trust and performance retargeting to drive visibility, qualified traffic, and online sales uplift.

Challenge Compete against Samsung/LG-level trust with a smaller budget.
Strategy Use creators to build trust first, then retarget warm audiences.
Outcome 100+ creators, 12M+ impressions, 180K+ visits, 22% sales uplift.

Business problem

The product had a strong value proposition, but the category was trust-heavy. Consumers already associated QLED with larger brands.

My thinking

For a high-consideration TV purchase, performance ads alone would not solve the trust gap. The better sequence was creator education first, retargeting second.

Execution

Creator tiers, review-led briefs, product demos, performance retargeting, offer-led CTAs, marketplace-led conversion paths, and launch reporting.

Experiments

Creator format, creator tier mix, retargeting creative, CTA copy, and destination testing between brand site and marketplace journeys.

What failed

Large creators did not always produce the best traffic. Feature-only ads lacked trust. Attribution was not perfectly linear.

Case study 03 · Cashify

Turning Cashify’s affiliate ecosystem into a growth channel.

Improved partner-led revenue through account segmentation, creator activation, offer coordination, and affiliate campaign discipline.

Challenge Affiliate revenue was active but under-optimized.
Strategy Segment partners, activate creators, improve campaign rhythm.
Outcome ₹12L+ monthly revenue, 22% uplift, 40% lift in partner-acquired customers.

Business problem

Cashify had a large partner ecosystem, but not every partner was equally activated. The growth opportunity was improving output from the existing base.

My thinking

I treated affiliate growth like account-based growth: identify which partners had headroom, which needed reactivation, and where creators could reduce trust friction.

Execution

Partner segmentation, creator outreach, offer-led campaigns, seasonal pushes, reporting, partner follow-ups, and revenue tracking.

Experiments

Partner upsell campaigns, creator explainer content, dormant partner reactivation, incentive variation, and seasonal campaign pushes.

What failed

Generic partner outreach had weak response. Some creators drove reach but not quality. Too many low-potential accounts diluted attention.

Operating system

How I approach growth.

I don’t think of growth as running campaigns. I think of it as building a system: find the constraint, choose the audience, design the funnel, test the message, read the data, then scale what survives.

01

Diagnose

Find the actual constraint: awareness, conversion, trust, retention, CAC, or repeatability.

02

Segment

Define ICPs, intent levels, audience quality, and buyer motivation.

03

Build

Create the funnel, message, landing journey, tracking, and reporting structure.

04

Experiment

Run controlled tests across channels, creative, offers, audiences, and CRO.

05

Scale

Move budget and effort toward what proves repeatable enough to fund.
