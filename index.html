Case study 01 · Aerohyre

Building India’s aviation hiring growth engine from zero.

Scaled a first-mover aviation job platform from 0 to 165K+ MAUs in 11 months by building the acquisition, analytics, SEO, and CRO engine from scratch.

ChallengeNo category awareness, no historical CAC, marketplace cold start.
StrategyAcquire job seekers first, build trust, then scale acquisition.
Outcome165K+ MAUs, 8K+ sign-ups, improved ROAS and CAC.

Business problem

Aerohyre was not just launching a product — it was creating a category. Aviation hiring was fragmented across generic job boards, referrals, and informal networks.

My thinking

The key decision was sequencing. I focused first on candidate liquidity because job-seeker traction would become the proof needed to later acquire employers.

Execution

Google Search, Meta, YouTube, retargeting, SEO landing pages, role-specific messaging, GA4/GTM tracking, UTM discipline, and weekly funnel reporting.

What failed

Broad-match keywords wasted spend early. Generic job-portal messaging felt undifferentiated. Cold campaigns without retargeting underperformed.

Case study 02 · Google QLED TV

Launching Google QLED TV with creators + performance marketing.

Built a launch motion using creator-led trust and performance retargeting to drive visibility, qualified traffic, and online sales uplift.

ChallengeCompete against Samsung/LG-level trust with a smaller budget.
StrategyUse creators to build trust first, then retarget warm audiences.
Outcome100+ creators, 12M+ impressions, 180K+ visits, 22% sales uplift.

Business problem

The product had a strong value proposition, but the category was trust-heavy. Consumers already associated QLED with larger brands.

My thinking

For a high-consideration TV purchase, performance ads alone would not solve the trust gap. The better sequence was creator education first, retargeting second.

Execution

Creator tiers, review-led briefs, product demos, performance retargeting, offer-led CTAs, marketplace-led conversion paths, and launch reporting.

What failed

Large creators did not always produce the best traffic. Feature-only ads lacked trust. Attribution was not perfectly linear.

Case study 03 · Cashify

Turning Cashify’s affiliate ecosystem into a growth channel.

The affiliate program had 50+ partners, but revenue was unevenly distributed. Growth was not a partner acquisition problem — it was an activation and prioritization problem.

ChallengeBottom 60% of accounts contributed less than 15% of total revenue.
StrategySegment partners like an ABM target list and prioritize headroom.
Outcome22% uplift primarily from 8 under-supported existing accounts.

The real problem

The affiliate program had 50+ partners but revenue was unevenly distributed — the bottom 60% of accounts contributed less than 15% of total revenue. Growth wasn't a partner acquisition problem, it was an activation and prioritization problem.

The insight

I treated the affiliate base like an ABM target list. Segmented all 50+ partners into three tiers by revenue headroom. Tier 1 got dedicated support cadence, tailored offers, and creator content to reduce audience trust friction. Dormant accounts got personalized reactivation outreach that referenced their specific last performance gap — not a batch email.

Execution

Built partner tiers, prioritized accounts by revenue headroom, created tailored offer pushes, supported creator-led trust content, and maintained structured follow-up cadences for high-potential accounts.

What failed

Generic partner outreach had near-zero response. Batch commission update emails didn't move anyone. What worked was treating every reactivation like a sales motion — specific, referenced, and tied to a clear next action.

Outcome

The 22% revenue uplift came primarily from 8 existing accounts that had been under-supported, not from signing new partners.

Case study 04 · Tyroo · AdTech · B2B

Building SEO-led GTM positioning for a B2B AdTech platform across APAC.

B2B AdTech content either over-indexes on technical jargon or is too generic to differentiate. The gap was content that could both rank for buying-intent keywords and give the sales team something credible to use in pipeline conversations across BFSI, Retail, and Gaming verticals.

ChallengeDifferentiate B2B AdTech content beyond jargon and generic thought leadership.
StrategyBuild keyword clusters mapped to vertical-specific buyer pain points.
Outcome85+ keywords ranked and $250K+ pipeline influenced.

The challenge

B2B AdTech content either over-indexes on technical jargon or is too generic to differentiate. The gap was content that could both rank for buying-intent keywords and give the sales team something credible to use in pipeline conversations across BFSI, Retail, and Gaming verticals.

The execution

Audited content gaps versus competitors. Built vertical-specific keyword clusters for each APAC industry segment. Developed positioning frameworks that mapped Tyroo's capabilities to specific buyer pain points per vertical. Prioritized pages by pipeline contribution potential, not just search volume.

The insight

B2B content fails when marketing and sales treat it separately. Every piece I built had both a keyword target and a sales-angle annotation — so it became a shared asset, not a marketing deliverable that sales ignored.

Operating system

How I approach growth.

I don’t think of growth as running campaigns. I think of it as building a system: find the constraint, choose the audience, design the funnel, test the message, read the data, then scale what survives.

01

Diagnose

Find the actual constraint: awareness, conversion, trust, retention, CAC, or repeatability.

02

Segment

Define ICPs, intent levels, audience quality, and buyer motivation.

03

Build

Create the funnel, message, landing journey, tracking, and reporting structure.

04

Experiment

Run controlled tests across channels, creative, offers, audiences, and CRO.

05

Scale

Move budget and effort toward what proves repeatable enough to fund.
